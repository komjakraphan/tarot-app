What is tarot?

A divination tool that uses a deck of 78 cards, each with symbolic imagery and meanings, to provide insight, guidance, and reflection on various aspects of life. The first known believed origin was a 15th century European card game, but some believe it is much older. Now, it has become associated with mysticism and fortune-telling.

The tarot deck

78 cards divided into 2 types Major and Minor Arcana. The Major Arcana cards depict the fool’s journey of self discovery white the minor Arcana cards depict daily life. Below is a quick overview of the deck and their meanings. Each card can have 2 meanings, an upright or reversed meaning which means there are 156 interpretations.

Major Arcana (22 Cards)

These cards tell the story of the fool’s journey and their path to self discovery. It illustrates the path of innocence to enlightenment, depicting challenges, growth and transformation.

The Fool (0) starts innocent and open, experiencing life’s joys and trials. Through growth and transformation, they develop wisdom and strength. Finally, after breaking through limitations, they reach enlightenment with The World (XXI). This cycle repeats throughout life, with every challenge bringing us closer to self-awareness.

  1. Self-Discovery & Personal Power (Cards 0–VII)

    👉 Theme: Learning about oneself and the world, developing skills, and understanding personal strengths.

    • 0 – The Fool 🎒: A new journey begins—innocence, curiosity, unlimited potential.
    • I – The Magician ✨: The power of manifestation and action.
    • II – The High Priestess 🔮: Developing intuition and inner wisdom.
    • III – The Empress 🌿: Creativity, nurturing, and abundance.
    • IV – The Emperor 🏛️: Structure, stability, and authority.
    • V – The Hierophant 📜: Tradition, learning, and guidance.
    • VI – The Lovers ❤️: Choices, relationships, and values.
    • VII – The Chariot 🏇: Determination, self-discipline, and control.

    Lesson: Understanding one's power, intuition, relationships, and personal willpower.

  2. Growth Through Struggles & Transformation (Cards VIII–XIV)

    👉 Theme: Facing inner challenges, finding balance, and experiencing deep transformation.

    • VIII – Strength 🦁: Mastering emotions and inner strength.
    • IX – The Hermit 🔦: Solitude and deep self-reflection.
    • X – Wheel of Fortune 🎡: The ups and downs of fate.
    • XI – Justice ⚖️: Cause and effect, truth, and responsibility.
    • XII – The Hanged Man 🌀: Letting go, new perspectives, surrender.
    • XIII – Death ☠️: Transformation, endings, and renewal.
    • XIV – Temperance 🌊: Finding harmony and moderation.

    Lesson: Life is full of changes, requiring patience, reflection, and the ability to release and adapt.

  3. Liberation, Awakening, & Completion (Cards XV–XXI)

    👉 Theme: Breaking illusions, overcoming limitations, and reaching enlightenment.

    • XV – The Devil 😈: Facing addictions, materialism, and fears.
    • XVI – The Tower ⚡: Sudden upheaval, destruction of false beliefs.
    • XVII – The Star ✨: Renewal, hope, and inspiration.
    • XVIII – The Moon 🌙: Illusions, uncertainty, and deep intuition.
    • XIX – The Sun ☀️: Joy, confidence, and truth revealed.
    • XX – Judgment 🔔: Awakening, self-evaluation, and rebirth.
    • XXI – The World 🌍: Completion, fulfillment, and integration of all lessons.

    🔹 Lesson: By facing our shadows, we break free from limitations, awaken to our true selves, and complete our journey with wisdom.

The Four Suits of the Minor Arcana

Each suit consists of 14 cards:

  • Ace–10 → A progression of experiences within that suit.
  • Court Cards (Page, Knight, Queen, King) → Different personalities, energies, or roles people play

Each suit is linked to an element, a realm of experience, and a symbolic association:

Suit Element Realm Themes
Wands 🔥 Fire Passion, creativity, ambition Inspiration, action, personal growth
Cups 💧 Water Emotions, relationships, intution Love, connection, inner feelings
Swords 🌬️ Air Thoughts, intellect, conflict Logic, communication, challenges
Pentacles 🌍 Earth Material world, career, stability Wealth, home, physical well-being

WANDS (FIRE): Passion, Ambition, & Creativity

The Suit of Wands represents energy, drive, and the spark of inspiration. It’s about dreams, goals, and personal growth, but also impulsiveness and burnout.

  • Ace of Wands → A new idea or creative spark.
  • 2–10 of Wands → The journey of ambition, from early excitement to potential overburden.
  • Court Cards → Passionate, bold, and action-oriented people.

Reversed Meaning: Lack of motivation, creative blocks, frustration.

CUPS (WATER): Emotions, Love, & Intuition

The Suit of Cups governs feelings, relationships, and inner fulfillment. It deals with love, friendships, intuition, and emotional highs and lows.

  • Ace of Cups → A new emotional experience, love, or deep connection.
  • 2–10 of Cups → The range of emotional experiences, from love to heartbreak to fulfillment.
  • Court Cards → Empathetic, emotional, and intuitive personalities.

Reversed Meaning: Emotional imbalance, repressed feelings, unrealistic expectations.

SWORDS (AIR): Intellect, Truth, & Challenges

The Suit of Swords represents thoughts, logic, and communication, but also conflict, mental struggles, and decision-making. It’s about the power of the mind—both its clarity and its ability to create anxiety.

  • Ace of Swords → A moment of clarity, truth, or an important decision.
  • 2–10 of Swords → The journey through challenges, from doubt to resolution.
  • Court Cards → Rational, analytical, and sometimes overly critical people.

Reversed Meaning: Miscommunication, mental blocks, self-deception.

PENTACLES (EARTH): Career, Stability, & Material Success

The Suit of Pentacles deals with the physical world—money, health, career, and security. It’s about building a stable foundation, but also warns against material obsession.

  • Ace of Pentacles → A new financial or career opportunity.
  • 2–10 of Pentacles → The journey of material growth, from effort to long-term stability.
  • Court Cards → Hardworking, grounded, and resourceful individuals.

Reversed Meaning: Financial struggles, lack of growth, imbalance between work and life.
